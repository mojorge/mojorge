About Daggers High

Daggers High is a board game about the competitive college admissions process.

What is Daggers High?

Daggers High is a board game about being a high school student battling through cutthroat college admissions.

How to Play

1. Play an Event Card from your hand

2. Take actions equal to the number in the top left corner

3. Win by having the most resume points!

Buy on The Game Crafter

I put Daggers High on The Game Crafter. Each time someone wants a copy of Daggers High, one will be printed on the spot. While it means no longer having the advantage of economy of scale, I was able to bring the costs down this way since it means not needing to make a print run of over 1000 copies in order to make the game. The cost comes out to about $37 before shipping, and you can find it here: https://www.thegamecrafter.com/games/daggers-highschool

Fair warning: this is an abridged version of the game. In order to keep costs down, several significant things had to be done:

  • No Game Box
  • Removed the FUN entirely (and all associated event cards)
  • Removed Essays entirely (and all associated event cards)
  • Removed Achievements entirely
  • Cut 6 Clubs, 6 Influence Cards, and 12 Event Cards
  • 25 small cubes instead of 36 per player. No medium cubes included.
  • Removed dice.
  • Removed special characters
  • Smaller player boards
  • I make 0 profit off of sales

If you want the full, unabridged version of the game, buy one from me through the link above!

Rules FAQ

  • Does it cost an action to use a Teacher Ability?

    • No. It does, however, cost teacher influence.

  • Do yearly Club Effects occur before Student Government President moves?

    • Yes. Yearly club effects occur at the end of the year, while Student Government President moves at the beginning of the year.

  • What if there is a tie in friends for Student Government President?

    • It stays with the person who currently owns it.

  • Does the effect of Senioritis kick in when played, or at any time?

    • At any time. So, a player who previously had less than 6 Happiness but later regained some can study again.
