Projects

This page is where I will document some of the interesting/fun projects I've worked on.

  • Interdimensional Worm Gold Rush by Jorge Zhang, Mark Agard, and Juan Gonzalez

    • Date: November 22, 2020

    This was made during a 3.5-hour coding competition. We made a game where you control a miner trying to get to gold, but a worm is trying to eat you while you do. Also, movement is difficult due to the inter dimensional interference that makes the buttons you press do unpredictable things. I made a similar game in Javascript that you can play here.

  • Walter Payton College Prep the Video Game by Jorge Zhang

    • Date: 2018

    This was based off of one of the very first iterations of Daggers High. It is a single player text-based adventure where you go through 4 years of high school and try to graduate without mentally breaking down. It is still clunky and probably buggy, but you can play through the whole game if you had the determination to (and not drop out)! I ended up recoding the entire game in Javascript and removing most references to Payton, and you can play it here.

  • Real Rank by Jorge Zhang and David Hafner

    • Date: 2017

    We thought that US News and Report didn't do justice to properly rank Illinois high schools, so we decided to do it ourselves! Made on the principle that every individual is looking for something different, and so the rankings adjust based off of a few criteria. Find it here.

  • Puddle by Jorge Zhang, David Hafner, Lucas Pauker, and four people we met randomly

    • Date: 2016

    This was made during a 1-day coding competition called Chicago Code Day. The idea was something that would write your own English essays for you: we failed miserably, but our results were hilarious! So instead of branding ourselves as an automatic essay writer, we made it into a comedic short story generator called Puddle.
