]]> By: Jorge Zhang https://jorgezhang.com/2020/03/universities-must-adopt-permanent-and-universal-as/#comment-8658 Mon, 30 Mar 2020 00:41:53 +0000 https://jorgezhang.com/?p=5378#comment-8658 In reply to Zachary Moore.

Thanks Zach! The terminology “Universal A” and “Double A” might be specific to Georgetown, though people have been calling to abolish grades for quite a while. Even more generally than grades, studies have shown that rewards seem to restrict creativity. As it turns out, grading for “creativity” also doesn’t work! As soon as students have found a creative idea/twist that seems to result in a higher creativity score, they stick within the confines of that idea. Ultimately, the reason you cannot grade creativity is because true creativity requires the possibility of failure. Yet, students aren’t going to take that risk of failure if their grade is depending on it.
