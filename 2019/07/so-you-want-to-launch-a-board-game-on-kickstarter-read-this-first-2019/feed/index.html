I would say that although your project didn’t fund this time, it was worth the experience for the wealth of knowledge you now have. I am also an aspiring game creator who has spent a lot of time researching Kickstarter and working on projects for that platform. I would definitely recommend reading Jamey Stegmaier’s book “A Crowdfunder’s Strategy Guide” several times before launching a project. I would add to what Jorge has written by saying that while running a Kickstarter project will cost money, you can mitigate many of the costs by a) including said costs in your funding goal so you can pay yourself back later, and b) spending lots of time beforehand building your community and promoting yourself to fellow gamers. As Richard Bliss says in his Kickstarter podcast, “you don’t have a funding problem, you have a CROWD problem.” Thanks again, Jorge!
