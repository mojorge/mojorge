The spam folder might be hard to bypass without your recipients manually marking the emails you send as “not spam.” If you have first names/last names associated with each email, you have the option to “Personalize the ‘to:’ field.” This helps avoid spam filters since the recipient’s name will be attached to the emails you send. It also makes the emails feel more personal, which could help with your open rate. At the end of the day, the open rate can be misleading anyway if your recipients have disabled the automatic downloading of images (like if they have an android phone). Hope this helps!
